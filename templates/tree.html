Classification Decision Tree

Here, the decision tree is used to identify which variable has the most impact in making customers exit. Below is the tree chart for the current dataset.

After the tree chart is constructed, one can estimate the impact power for each of the variables by summing changes in the mean squared error and dividing the sum by the number of branch nodes. Below shows the normalized impact power for the decision tree and compared to the regression model.

It can be seen that two different models yielded different conclusions. Both models concluded "Age" has the most impact while the regression predicted "Active Member" to be the 2nd most impactful variable ; on the other hand, the decision tree predicted "Number of product" to be the 2nd most impactful variable. A proper analysis is often needed to determine which model is most suited for the particular dataset. In this case, the regression model did not predict "Number of product" has higher impact than other variables.
